The Electrification Journey

Aston Martin is pursuing a pragmatic, phased "blended drivetrain approach" to electrification, with Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) serving as a technological bridge to full Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs), while strategically continuing its iconic Internal Combustion Engine (ICE) heritage.

Electrification Roadmap Highlights:

H2 2025: Valhalla PHEV Supercar

Deliveries commence for the first series-production mid-engined PHEV, incorporating F1-derived technology.

By 2026: Electrified Option for All New Lines

All new Aston Martin product lines will offer an electrified powertrain option (PHEV or BEV).

~2026-2028: First BEV Launch

Target launch for the first Aston Martin BEV, likely an SUV, leveraging Lucid Group powertrain technology.

By 2030: Fully Electrified Core Portfolio

The entire core Sport/GT and SUV portfolio will be electrified.

Aston Martin will continue V12 and V8 ICE production for as long as feasible, potentially utilizing low-volume exemptions, reflecting current market demand and brand DNA.

Grafana can monitor BEV/PHEV performance, battery health, charging infrastructure uptime, and the efficiency of the electrification rollout.

Projected Powertrain Mix (Next 5 Years)

Illustrating the continued significance of ICE and Hybrid technologies in the near term.

CEO Adrian Hallmark estimates ~85% of sales will be ICE-based (pure or hybrid) in the next five years.

Powering Performance: The F1 Tech Nexus & Cloud

The Aston Martin Aramco Formula One™ Team serves as a crucible for innovation, heavily leveraging High-Performance Computing (HPC), AI, and advanced data analytics, with significant technology transfer to road cars. Cloud technologies are pivotal in this high-octane environment.

F1 as an Innovation Incubator:

  • Valkyrie hypercar merges F1® technology with a road car.
  • Valhalla PHEV incorporates learnings and technologies from F1.
  • Aerodynamic advancements influence future road models, including EVs.
  • "Aston Martin Performance Technologies" division facilitates tech transfer.
